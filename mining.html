In this section we will cover the basics of mining and how to make some extra money in Elite.

Mining an asteroid field

Mining

Mining in elite is a potentially long and relatively fruitless exercise in Elite if you do not bring the correct equipment. This section will look at what you need to mine and how to go about it.

Equipment/Process
  • Mining laser (required)

    A mining laser allows you to mine asteroids. You simply aim and shoot. Chunks of ore will then come off the asteroid

  • Refinery (required)

    A refinery allows you to process the ore into cargo. The larger the refinery, the more types of ore you can refine at one time. I'd strongly advise to go as big as you sensibly can.

  • Cargo (required)

    The more cargo space you have the greater the amount you can store at once

  • Pulse Wave Analyser (recommended)

    This will higlight in yellow any asteroids that have rarer deposits. There is no ammo to this so it can be fired ad infinitum.

  • Collector Limpets (recommended)

    Collector limpets will collect the chunks of ore that come off the asteroid. You can just use your cargo hatch, but it will slow you down massively. Collector limpets can only deposit cargo if your hatch is open

  • Prospector Limpets (recommended)

    Prospector limpets are fired at asteroids and will let you know what ore is available and if there are any subsurface deposits. They will also let you know if the asteroid can be cracked. The other advantage they have is that they double the amount of ore available from the asteroid once they attach.

  • Abrasion Blaster

    Once you have used a prospector limpet, you will see surface deposits that can be blown off the asteroid. It is possible to see these deposits without a prospector, but it is not as easy.

  • Sub-Surface Displacement Missile

    This 'drill-on-a-missile' allows you to mine out deposits under the surface of the asteroid. You need to have used a prospector to know where these are. Can yield rarer elements in larger quantities.

  • Seismic Charges

    This is how you 'crack' an asteroid. Once you have fired a prospector limpet, you may see unstable fissures. By firing seismic charges into these fissures you can break the asteroid apart and get a lot of resources from it. However, too much charge and you will destroy some of the resources. Too little, and you will not break the asteroid. Definitely for veteran pilots.
